Basics of A USIS Audio Visual Wiring Schematic

While looking at different examples of past drawing packages will help in starting the USIS standard(s), below are some key points that need to be known when starting the process for the first time.

The Numbering Scheme

One of the key standardizations is of the numbering conventions  of all cables within a system. Below are the used standards as of today. Some flexibility can be given when considering large deployments and the potential for duplicated truncations.

 WIRING NUMBERING EXAMPLE

Example 1 = S01 | Example 2 = L1001

Above are examples of what US Information Systems Audio Visual cable numbers might look like on drawings and in the field labeled on cables.

 

Example 1 = S01

The two digit number represents a field cable wire  number.

The “S” represents Speaker audio level signal

The “01” represents Speaker cable one(1)

 

Example 1 = L1001

The three digit number represents a rack cable wire number.

The “L” represents Line level audio signal

The “001” represents Speaker cable one(1)

 

US Information Systems Audio Visual Cable Prefix Standard

C = Control Cable

D = Data

DAN = Dante Digital Audio

L = Line Level Audio

M = Microphone Level Audio

P = Power Cable

RF = Any Wireless RF device/ receiver  or Cable TV incoming feed

S = Speaker Level Audio

T = Telco (VoIP or POTS)

SP = Spare

V = Video Signal (All are generalized into “V”)
