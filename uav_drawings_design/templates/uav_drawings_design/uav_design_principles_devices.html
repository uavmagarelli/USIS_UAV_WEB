UAV Design Principles - Device Locations Landing Page

Camera Locations

Camera(s) mounted under a display should aim for center of lens to be 48” Above Finished Floor Bring Your Own Camera locations should include a power requirement next to the location. Camera locations that utilize transmitter / receiver kits should not call for power unless the transmitting device needs power that can not be stored remotely.

  

Conduit

40% Fill—We aim for 40% capacity fill as per industry standard. Special considerations can be made on a  pipe by pipe basis. As a whole we should not deviate from asking for the appropriate size that lets us met  this criteria.

Never exceed 2 right angle turns per pull locations

 

Display Locations—General

By default we typically start with two(2) client LAN drops at each display locations

 

Display Locations—Wall Mounted

By default we specify in wall equipment storage boxes at every wall mounted display. The current desired Make and Model is Chief Mfg [Legrand AV] PAC526. Ensure you get the full kit with the ap propriate color faceplate.

Locations that are both wall mounted and do not have in-wall storage poxes than we should be calling for  clock style power outlets.

Special consideration should be considered if multiple devices need to power at the display location.

 

Rack Location Power

Dedicated 20-Amp circuits are always the first line preference

Twist lock receptacles are always the first line preference

 

Projector Locations—Ceiling Mounted

There should be a maximum of 12” of threaded rod from the ceiling substrate to the pipe mount ing flange / unistrut.

The concept is that the finished 1.5” NTP pipe looks cleaner coming down the ceiling than fou(4) pieces of  5/8” threaded rod.

Shock mount kits should be used at all times. Basic Mason 5C141 vibrations shocks will be sufficient.

Ensure the shock mount utilize can support the weight of the project + the weight of all the  mounting hardware.

 

Speakers—Ceiling Mounted

Ceiling mounted speakers should have a safety chain / aircraft wire securing each speaker to the ceiling substrate or a  building ceiling supporting structure.

Wall Mounted Audio or Video Equipment

Wall mounted AV input locations should include a power requirement next to the location.
