Calculating The Min. Power Requirement

The simple way to do this is to add together all the Tap setting of each speaker in the chain

In this example we have speaker 1 being tapped at 7 Watts and speaker 2 being tapped at 7 Watts.

This would give us the following 7+7 = 14 Watts

It is considered good practice to leave 25% of an amplifiers overall Watts unused

This would give us the following (7+7) + 25% = 17.7 Watts

Calculating The Impedence

To calculate the impedance you use the formula: Impedance = Volts2/Power where Volts is the type of distribution system and power equals the min power requires of teh overall speaker chain.

We start with the Volts type of the distribution amplifier. For our example we will say 100v

In this example we have five(5) speakers tapped at 4 Watts each.

This would give Power (aka Watts) = 5 * 4 = 20

Now to tie it together we (volts*volts)/power

Giving us (100 * 100) / 20 = 500 Ω
